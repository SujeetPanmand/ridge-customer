What You Get!

How Much Meat?

Loafers Glory offers 1/8, 1/4, 1/2, or whole beef options. 1/8 beef is 50 lbs, 1/4 beef is 100 lbs, 1/2 beef is 200 lbs. of beef. A whole beef is 400 lbs. of beef. You can expect about 25% grilling steaks, 45% ground beef, 30% briskets, ribs, roasts, and stew meat.

How Much Freezer Space Do I Need?

Many of our customers will split a ½ Beef with friends and/or family. We call this Cow-Pooling! The rule of thumb is 1 cubic foot of freezer space for every 30-35 lbs of cut and wrapped meat.

  • 1/8 Beef (50 lbs.) = 1.7 cubic feet of freezer space.
  • ¼ Beef (100 lbs.) = 3.4 cubic feet of freezer space.
  • ½ Beef (200 lbs.) = 6.8 cubic feet of freezer space.
  • Whole Beef (400 lbs.) = 14 cubic feet of freezer space

How to pick your cuts?

Loafers Glory has the option to customize your cuts for ½ and whole beef orders. This is where you can choose whether you want your Ribeye’s to have the bone in or out, how thick your steaks should be, whether your brisket is kept whole for your smoker or cut to fit your crockpot, etc. If you’re unsure, just ask! We have a standard cut sheet you can start withor are at least happy to help guide you. Be sure to ask about organ meat if you don’t want it. Many producers will require you to buy all the cuts that the animal produces,including organ meat. At Loafers Glory, we offer the organ meat separately.

How is it packaged?

At Loafers Glory you get individual cuts of beef vacuum-sealed in properly USDA labeled, airtight packages. That way you can easily inspect the meat before you thaw it.If it is airtight, it will last for 2 or more years in your freezer.
